Decision record 001

Keep the shot semantics; remove the wasted pixels.

The original recommendation said “exact grid-cell DDA/supercover.” The audit found that a conventional geometric DDA or strict supercover is not behavior-compatible with the shipped engine. The accepted implementation is therefore a closed-form, cell-jumping traversal of the exact historical integer Bresenham raster.

Accepted

Discrete grid-jump traversal

Jump directly to the next grid boundary while analytically reproducing every integer Bresenham coordinate that could be the first sample in a cell.

Rejected

Thin geometric DDA

It drops legacy corner-alias cells for large-unit origins and substitutes geometric boundary coordinates for the first raster pixel.

Rejected

Strict supercover

It emits both side-adjacent cells when a line touches a corner, creating new blockers, hits, splash anchors, and Through Shot stops.

Compatibility contract

For every finite, engine-range ray—where rounded coordinates and the closed-form integer products stay inside JavaScript’s exact-integer range—output the same ordered [cell, firstPosition] sequence as the removed pixel walker. This preserves the first unit or mountain, falloff divisor, Through Shot order, Wingshield termination, Area Throw interception, affected-cell order, obstacle animation position, and AI candidate signatures.

One intentional malformed-input change

Non-finite endpoints now fail closed with an empty result. The legacy loop could fail to advance forever for NaN or Infinity. Finite in-engine behavior is unchanged.

Architecture correction

a13 is not currently a 12-ply minimax tree. It branches at the root, then both sides follow their configured policies through each rollout. Classic alpha–beta pruning has nothing to prune after the root until the engine deliberately introduces adversarial branching.

Keep the three deadline surfaces separate

The common 175 ms deadline is checkpointed, not preemptive: one expensive operation can overshoot before the next check and fall back to the incumbent. The common 275 ms circuit is also post-hoc and starts after early classification/state scans. Ranked wraps ensureBotSearchDriver() + chooseDecision() with a coarse Date.now() timer and opens its separate circuit only when elapsed time is strictly greater than 300 ms; the just-computed decision still executes. Reports therefore retain common fallback, common circuit, and ranked circuit classifications independently.

Workstream 1 · deep dive

Exact grid-cell traversal of the legacy shot

The removed implementation allocated one position object for every world pixel along a shot, then mapped and deduplicated those positions into cells. Real rays averaged roughly 928–1,434 raster pixels but only 12–17 emitted cells. The replacement calculates the next transition directly.

Old cost model

  1. Round the start and end coordinates.
  2. Walk Bresenham one integer pixel at a time.
  3. Allocate and retain every {x, y} position.
  4. Map each position back to a grid cell.
  5. Linearly search a key array to deduplicate cells.

Complexity follows world-pixel distance and creates avoidable allocation.

New cost model

  1. Round endpoints exactly as before.
  2. Represent Bresenham coordinates in closed form.
  3. Invert the monotone displacement at each cell boundary.
  4. Jump to the minimum next-axis transition.
  5. Emit only the exact first legacy pixel in each new cell.

Complexity follows crossed cells and allocates only observable output.

Proof sketch and boundary rules

Let M = max(|Δx|, |Δy|), let A be one axis extent, and let k be a major-axis step. The historical strict Bresenham tie behavior is reproduced by: 

dA(k) = floor((2 · k · A + M − 1) / (2 · M))

firstStep(required) = ceil((2 · M · required − M + 1) / (2 · A))

For positive travel, a cell begins at ceil(upperBoundary). For negative travel it begins at ceil(lowerBoundary) − 1. The minus one is not cosmetic: it preserves the one-pixel interior bias that the obstacle remap and range falloff already observe. At every iteration the helper jumps to min(nextX, nextY) and calculates the exact legacy point.

Why geometric DDA is a gameplay change

Large units originate on a grid vertex. Depending on tie policy, conventional thin DDA changed 22.3–23.0% of legal large-unit direct paths and 30.2% of projected Through Shot paths. A strict supercover changes more by adding both cells at corner contact.

Example: a large origin at base vertex (1,1) shooting toward target (2,6), UP, historically visits 1,1 → 1,2 → 1,3 → 1,4 → 2,4 → 2,5 → 2,6 → 2,7. A geometric DDA drops 2,4, so a unit or mountain there would stop intercepting the shot.

Why exact geometric intersections are also unsafe

Even when small-origin cell order matches, the first entry position differs on 85.4% of legal direct rays and 87.3% of Through rays, by up to 16 pixels. One audited range-5 ray enters the same cell at legacy pixel (−896,700), distance 639.21 and divisor 1; geometric DDA reports (−896,704), distance 643.19 and divisor 2. Damage changes.

Tradeoffs and side effects

Positive

  • Work scales with crossed cells rather than world pixels.
  • Intermediate pixel arrays and the second mapping pass disappear.
  • Fewer temporary objects reduce GC pressure in rollout-heavy matches.
  • The helper is isolated, pure for engine-range inputs, and directly fuzzable.
  • Malformed non-finite rays terminate safely.

Costs and retained debt

  • The arithmetic is less obvious than textbook DDA and needs its proof comments/tests.
  • It intentionally preserves legacy corner aliases, directional asymmetry, and y-origin behavior.
  • It preserves (x << 4) | y out-of-grid key collisions for exact parity.
  • Very large coordinates still rely on JavaScript Number precision; engine ranges are small.
  • Non-zero 1–4 px rays pay more constant overhead; the measured crossover is between 4 and 8 px, far below ordinary combat spans.
  • The exhaustive differential file adds about 8.1 seconds locally; its heaviest case has a 60-second per-test allowance for slower CI hosts.
  • A future grid rule change must update the oracle and helper together.

Deferred cleanup is deliberate

Replacing the 16-wide packed key, changing out-of-grid treatment, using mathematical entry points, or making corner contact a true supercover may each be desirable game-rule changes. They must be proposed and balanced separately; combining them with a performance patch would make regressions impossible to attribute.

Why this slice

A primitive fingerprint scan is still O(units + effects), but it is far cheaper than cleaning, rebuilding, deduplicating, and reapplying every relation. It also avoids a distributed invalidation-hook matrix while the mutation surface is still changing. Dirty calls remain full refreshes, with only static geometry reuse; a later per-emitter delta system must beat this simpler qualified base.

Split timing makes the tradeoff concrete: an invalidated refresh is about 10.43% slower, while a true no-op is about 92.06% cheaper. The simple median model crosses over near a 10.18% no-op rate. Why this boundary

The catalog owns one synchronous decision, not a logical state key. That lifetime is sufficient for the measured duplicate request and makes invalidation structural: after any action, mutation, restore, or next policy turn, no later lookup consults the old catalog. The same-value restored state still gets an independently owned result.

Rejected broader designs

Generic range-ray and visible-edge caches were exact but failed the bookkeeping share gate. A PathHelper subclass violated the public fresh-result ownership contract. Deep-cloning every cache hit cost more than recomputation, so only internal consumers receive the readonly view and mutable application state takes an explicit boundary copy.

TypeScript readonly is not a runtime freeze. The production contract therefore forbids in-place mutation of the admitted occupancy or aggression matrix during one synchronous decision. A future consumer that needs such mutation must create a new catalog or bypass it. Never extend this candidate into a state-keyed/global cache: no entry may survive apply/restore or enter a sibling rollout.

Branch elimination

What can be skipped safely

“This branch looks bad” is useful only if it is either a proof or an explicitly measured heuristic. Ranked search must keep those categories separate, with exact pruning enabled first and heuristic pruning evaluated as a new AI policy.

Exact rollout-mean bound

Leaf scores lie in [0,1]. After k of R rollouts with sum S: 

lower = S / R
upper = (S + R − k) / R

A challenger may stop only when upper cannot beat the incumbent under the current gate, tie rule, and selector mode. An incumbent may stop only when its lower bound already defeats every challenger’s upper bound.

Why gains may initially be small

Production uses only two rollouts per candidate. After one score, the unfinished interval is still one-half wide, so few ordinary comparisons can be resolved. The same bound becomes more useful if validation rollouts or deeper stochastic sampling grows.

Lexicographic modes—productive fallback, dominant finish, urgency, target pressure, aggressive wait ties—and audit/IL completeness need dedicated rules or pruning disabled.

Permanent measurement protocol

Make improvements attributable

Microbenchmarks answer “did the primitive get cheaper?” CPU profiles answer “where did time move?” and paired game runs answer “did real decisions get faster without changing?” These are separate experiments and should not be collapsed into one percentage.

Primitive benchmark

  • Deterministic corpus with normal, Through, small, large, short, and long rays.
  • Warm both paths, then alternate old/new ordering for at least 11 trials.
  • Consume a checksum; report median, p90, time/ray, allocations if available.
  • Informational only in CI; never assert wall time.

Decision latency

  • One fixed maxLaps=2 warm-up match in every worker isolate, producing at least 20 complete decisions before recording.
  • 210 roster/map pair identities × two physical side swaps = 420 matches per variant.
  • Baseline/candidate pairing therefore yields 420 matched observations and 840 match executions per concurrency pass.
  • Concurrency 1 is the attribution pass; repeat at 4 and 12 only for saturation.
  • Report nearest-rank p50/p95/p99/p99.9/max and microseconds per simulated turn.

CPU profiles

  • Three independent fixed-work captures per variant at a 500 µs sampling interval.
  • Each capture repeats the frozen six-match corpus nine times: 3,249 accepted actions; 60–90 seconds is only the expected sizing range, never a stop condition.
  • Same source revision, environment, seeds, maps, rosters, and action count.
  • Aggregate functions by source scope; retain raw profiles.
  • Check Amdahl consistency before attributing a macro speedup.
