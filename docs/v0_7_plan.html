§1Why v0.6 is at its ceiling

Five structural limits, each evidenced in the ledger. The optimizer (CEM) is fine — everything around it binds.

  1. The policy class cannot express new behavior. 56 linear weights re-rank only candidates v0.4's heuristics emit. Every multi-pp win in history was a new play shape (hourglass +6–7pp, splash dispersion +5.58pp), never a weight movement. Resurrection, Wind Flow, Castling, area-throw, defend, morale/luck carry zero probability.
  2. One bit of credit per ~60-turn match. Selection SE ≈ 1.07pp at 2,200 games/candidate — at sub-pp headroom, CEM ranks candidates at random. Gains per run: +9.7 → +0.9 → +0.97 → 0.
  3. Frozen-anchor self-play is saturated & non-transitive. ~39% of mirrors are seat-decided; mirror ceiling ≈ 72–73%; beat-the-champion vectors that lose to v0.4 are documented.
  4. Both sides share the blind spots. A mechanic neither side uses reads as noise. Three multi-pp fixes baked after the "all levers exhausted" verdict.
  5. The sim certifies a game nobody plays. No live bot seat exists; live stacks are ceil(1000/exp) vs trained {50,30,15,8}; takeover ran pre-v0.5 findTarget; placement was a random scatter; the client truncated multi-action turns; live draft ran untrained weights (champion beats them 97.6%).
